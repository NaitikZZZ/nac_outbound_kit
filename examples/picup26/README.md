## Call Scripts

Two dial attempts: Day 5 (29 Apr) and Day 9 (3 May).

### Pre-call prep (30 sec)
- Confirm prospect's company segment: bank, NBFC, fintech, insurance, or vendor
- Check LinkedIn for any PICUP recap post
- Check Smartlead for email-open status

### Call 1 - Day 5

**Opening:**
> "Hi {{first_name}}, this is [YOUR NAME] from Xoxoday. Have I caught you at a bad time?"

**Purpose:**
> "Quick reason for the call. I work with banks and fintechs in India on the rewards and loyalty side of customer engagement, the bit that sits on top of UPI, CLOU, and the AI underwriting work most teams are doing. Wanted to get your perspective on how {{company_name}} handles it today."

**Discovery (4 questions):**
1. "Quick one, does customer rewards or loyalty sit anywhere near your charter, or is it on the marketing or product side?"
2. "How is it handled today - in-house build, point vendor, or manual gift card procurement?"
3. "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much of a priority is customer engagement infrastructure for {{company_name}} this year?"
4. "Who would be the right person internally if not you - Marketing, CX, Product, Loyalty?"

**Pitch (30 sec):**
> "Three things we do that are relevant. One, Plum, an API for rewards delivery, used by HDB, Fino, AU Small Finance. Two, Loyalife, a full multi-tier consumer loyalty platform. Three, Compass, agent and channel commission automation if {{company_name}} runs distribution partners. RBI-compliant Indian catalogue, SOC 2, ISO 27001."

**CTA:**
> "Two asks. If rewards or loyalty is on the roadmap, can we set up 15 minutes next week? If not, can I get the right name internally? I will mention you."

### Call 2 - Day 9
Only if no pickup on Call 1.

> "Hi {{first_name}}, [YOUR NAME] from Xoxoday again. Sent a voicemail and a couple of emails last week about rewards and loyalty post PICUP Fintech. Last try by phone before I stop. Better time now?"

## SDR Quick Notes

1. Reference PICUP Fintech in the first 15 seconds of every call. This is post-event, not cold.
2. Audience is senior (Manager+ to VP). Tone is peer-to-peer, not transactional.
3. The ask is often an intro, not a meeting. If they point you to the right person internally, that is a win.
4. Skip the feature dump. Three product names in one line, then listen.
5. Prioritise the 4 with valid emails for email + LinkedIn. Use phone for the 2 PNB and BCG leads (high value).
6. Log every call in HubSpot. Dashboard splits attribution by channel.
7. No em dashes or en dashes in any written follow-up. Use hyphens, commas, or rewrite.
8. No Smartlead email signatures in the body - Smartlead account-level signatures handle it.
